}} Chinese film directed by Jia Zhangke. The film is named after a popular song about waiting at a railway platform.

It is set in and around the small city of Fenyang, Shanxi province, China, from the end of the 1970s to the beginning of the 1990s. Fenyang was also the birthplace of director Jia Zhangke. The film is called "an epic of grassroots".

It follows a group of twenty-something performers as they face personal and societal changes.

Dialogue is a mixture of local speech, mainly Jin Chinese and Standard Chinese|Mandarin.

The film was voted the second best film of the past decade by the  , was voted the third best film.   Platform placed 32 on Slant Magazines list of the 100 best films of the 2000s  and was named as one of Sight & Sound’s films of the 2000s.
