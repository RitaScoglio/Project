==Reception==
The film does not contain action in the traditional sense, but it portrays, through an unusual mixture of documentary film|documentary-style modules and fiction, the conflicts in Cuban society that remain unsolved today.	
	
As De Cierta Manera reveals, Gómez was a revolutionary   community, its cultural traditions (including Abakuá and Santería), womens issues, the treatment of marginalized sectors of society, and the role of family within the context of the revolution and workers rights.  For its time, the film was extremely radical both in form and content.  Hence, Sara Gómez remains one of the most significant filmmakers from Latin America.
