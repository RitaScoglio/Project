==Plot summary==
A barrister (Robertson Hare) attempts to discourage his daughters infatuation for a philanderer by revealing his past. The plan backfires when the daughters would-be father-in-law (Stanley Holloway) threatens to reveal the barristers shady background.

==Notes==
In addition to the film featuring early appearances from future stars Audrey Hepburn and Roger Moore,  the role of Cherrie (June Sylvaine) was played, in the stage and film version, by the wife of the author (Vernon Sylvaine).  
 West End run, the Garrick Theatre and two cast members were featured in a humorous cameo scene, reading The Stage newspaper (probably looking for new jobs due to Londons anticipated destruction), in the 1950 film Seven Days to Noon (see still).
