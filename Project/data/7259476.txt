The Werewolf (1913) is a silent film short that is considered the first werewolf film and the earliest film of the Universal Monsters series. It was directed by Henry MacRae, produced by Bison Film Company, and released by Universal Studios. The script is by Ruth Ann Baldwin, based on the short story "The Werewolves" by Henry Beaugrand. It is now considered a lost film,  all prints supposedly having been destroyed in a 1924 fire at Universal Studios.

== Plot == Navajo woman, transforms into wolf in order to carry out vengeance against the invading white settlers. Then, 100 years after Watumas death, she returns from the dead to kill again.
